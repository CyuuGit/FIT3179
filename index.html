The Black Summer of Australia

The 2019-2020 Australian Bushfire Season, also known as the Black Summer, was a period of intense bushfires that raged throughout Australia. The fires started in June 2019, burning over 17 million hectares of land, destroying 3000 homes, killing or displacing approximately 3 billion animals, and claiming 33 lives. With the assistance of international aid, the final fires were extinguished early May 2020, ending the yearlong tragedy. Click on the image to learn more about the Black Summer.

This visualisation explores the impact that the Black Summer had on the individual states and cities of Australia, as well as provide a visual demonstration of how deadly these bushfires can be.

Analysis of Forest Damage

The Australian Department of Agriculture, Water and the Environment (ABARES) estimated a total fire area of 10,331,000 hectares. Within the total fire area, 83% of the area burned was forested land. This resulted in approximately 8,341,000 hectares of forest land succumbing to the fires.

Temperature Analysis

Bushfires are known to occur more frequently during summer as this is when the ambient temperature registers HIGHESTas high temperatures allow fuels to be closer to its ignition point and pre-heated fuel loads burn faster. Dry air intensifies fires at low humidity because plants release their moisture more easily, leaving them dryer and more suited for ignition.
Winds can blow fires into the direction of fresh fuel, igniting and spreading the flames whilst providing a continuous supply of oxygen. Wind speeds around 12-15 km/h have significant differences in behaviour of bushfires, even a slight increase can drastically increase the fires behaviour and advancement.

Change in Air Quality

Smoke, a by-product of bushfires, contributes to the pollution levels as Carbon Dioxide and soot is released into the atmosphere. PM2.5 particles (diameter less than 2.5 micrometres) are very small particles that are usually found in smoke increase the air pollution levels, degrading the air quality.
